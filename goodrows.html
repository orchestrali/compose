Finding Good Rows

according to Alison Stevens

Categories

When I look at a row that I think is good, I am interested in trying to identify why it's good, and if there are other rows that share good features with it. After all, in a composition on seven or more bells I can only include each good row once! So it's useful to look for categories of good rows. I do often start with an individual nice row and build a category or two from there.

Each row here has the bell pairs in the order 15 26 37 48, but each pair can be forward or reversed.

I wouldn't try to argue that all or even any of these rows is as good as Tittums. It's just that they don't belong to the category of unremarkable or bad rows either! I particularly like that some of these rows have pairs of consecutive bells together, like 15623784.

If I were forced to rank rows within this category, my decisions would mostly be based on repetition or regularity. Tittums itself is 15 repeated lower three times, and also 1526 repeated lower once. The row 51627384 has these same two levels of repetition. None of the other rows have consistency of the two-bell segments, but some maintain the repetition from first half to second half, like 51267348. In some the second half is like a mirror image of the first half: 51623748 and 15627348 are examples of this. Both repetition and mirroring of first and second half require the row to be two call changes away from Tittums. I personally find these rows to be slightly nicer than the "near misses" of Tittums (one call change away).

Other Tittums relatives

Speaking of near misses of Tittums, there are some not in the list above that are pretty great, like 15263478 and 12563748. These are pretty commonly rung in call changes on the way to and from Tittums. They are also near misses of 12563478, apparently called Hagdyke. Near misses of rounds are widely recognized as good but a lot of near misses of other named rows are also pretty good, particularly ones that are near misses of two different named rows!

Another way I like to expand the Tittums category is finding rows with Tittums on a set of six bells. On major the options are: 142536, 253647, 364758, and 162738 (stretched Tittums! or as much of Tittums on ten as is possible! On major I personally prefer this one reversed, 837261).

Ranking runs

Runs are some of the most obvious nice things. I think when most ringers consider which runs are better than others, the important factors are length and whether the run is at the front or back vs middle.

A big consideration for me is tonal implications of runs. Runs stand out in ringing because the small intervals help our brains connect the bells into a single unit. Given a run, the first and last bell will be a bit more prominent, boundaries of a space that the run fills. On eight bells, a 5432 run outlines the interval of a tritone! Tritones are really important in music, but we often expect them to behave in specific ways. Sticking with bell numbers on eight, the expected pattern would be for 2 (the leading tone) to "resolve" to 1 (the tonic) and 5 to "resolve" to 6. Rows ending 2345 and rows ending 3456 might seem to have similar value—they all end with a four-bell run—but they will have a very different feel. I think there is a place for 2345s but given the choice I'd rather have 3456s, especially at backstroke.

The tritone stuff means I also like these patterns (on eight): 2516, 5261, 2561, 5216 (exploded tittumsy), 2156, 5621. On ten bells the tritone is between bells 4 and 7, and on twelve bells it's 2, 6, and 9.

3-bell...runs?

Three bells don't make much of a run, particularly on more than six bells total. But if a row has two or more three-bell runs, it could be a nice row! A common goal of call changes on ten is 3216549870 (called Rollercoaster), which could be described as three three-bell runs.

On major, there are 318 rows containing two 3-bell runs. I certainly wouldn't consider them all equally good, but I think more than just Rollercoaster (14327658) and 12354678 (a near miss of Rounds) should be recognized! Here are some of my favorites:
